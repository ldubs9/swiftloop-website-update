# Maintaining a Website: What Happens After Launch

There's a myth that a website is "done" when it launches. In reality, launch day is when the site starts its actual job — and like anything that works for a living, it needs upkeep. Here's what that really involves, so there are no surprises.

## The quiet essentials

- **Security and updates.** Software dependencies need patching. Skip this and you risk breakage or worse — a hacked site is a brand catastrophe.
- **Performance maintenance.** Speed can drift as content and images are added. Periodic tuning keeps Core Web Vitals green.
- **Backups and uptime.** Things go wrong. A maintained site has backups and monitoring so a problem is fixed before customers notice.
- **Fixing the small stuff.** A broken link, a form that stopped emailing, an image that won't load — minor alone, costly in aggregate.

## The growth work

Maintenance keeps the lights on. The real value is improvement:

- **Content updates** — new products, offers, pages, as your business moves.
- **Reading the data** — analytics show what's working. Acting on it is where conversion gains come from.
- **Tuning automations** — AI prompts and workflows want adjusting as your needs evolve.
- **Iterating on design** — small, evidence-led refinements that compound over time.

## Who does it?

Three options, honestly. Do it yourself (fine if you have the time and skill, risky if not). Call someone ad-hoc when something breaks (slow, stressful, and you're a stranger each time). Or keep a retainer — a studio that already knows your site, tending it continuously.
