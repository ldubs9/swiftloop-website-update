# Motion and 3D on the Web (Three.js / GSAP): When It's Worth It

The most memorable websites move. Interactive 3D, smooth scroll-driven animation, a product you can spin in your hand — these are the experiences that make people stop and remember a brand. They're also the easiest thing to get wrong. Done badly, motion is the fastest way to make a fast site feel slow.

## What these tools do

- **Three.js / WebGL** renders real 3D in the browser — a product you rotate and light, an immersive scene, a configurator. No plugin, no app.
- **GSAP** drives precise, smooth animation — type that reveals as you scroll, elements that respond to the cursor, transitions that feel intentional.

Together they turn a flat page into something closer to an experience.

## When it's worth it

- **You sell on feeling** — luxury, design, products where the experience *is* the pitch.
- **The motion has a job** — showing a product from every angle, guiding attention, explaining something static images can't.
- **You want to stand out** — in a sea of template sites, considered motion is an instant signal of craft and budget.

## When it isn't

- **When it's decoration** — movement for its own sake distracts and annoys.
- **When it costs speed** — heavy 3D that takes five seconds to load loses more than it wins. Performance is non-negotiable.
- **When it breaks usability** — if a visitor can't find the buy button under the spectacle, the spectacle failed.

## The discipline that makes it work

Great motion is restrained and *swift* — decisive eases, no bouncing, no floaty drift. It's engineered to stay fast: optimised assets, sensible loading, a graceful fallback on older devices, and respect for users who prefer reduced motion. The skill isn't adding effects. It's knowing which one earns its place and cutting the rest.

## How we think about it

We treat motion like the accent colour in a brand: powerful precisely because it's rare. One unforgettable interaction beats ten gratuitous ones — and it should never cost you a customer who just wanted to get something done.
