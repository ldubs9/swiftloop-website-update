# Why Core Web Vitals Matter for UAE Businesses

Three numbers decide whether your website feels fast or frustrating. Google calls them Core Web Vitals, and it uses them to rank you. Customers don't know the names — they just leave.

## The three signals, in plain terms

- **Largest Contentful Paint (LCP)** — how long until the main content appears. Aim for under 2.5 seconds.
- **Interaction to Next Paint (INP)** — how quickly the page responds when someone taps or clicks. Under 200 milliseconds feels instant.
- **Cumulative Layout Shift (CLS)** — how much the page jumps around as it loads. The less it shifts, the more trustworthy it feels.

## Why a UAE business should care

Two reasons, both measurable.

**Ranking.** Google folds these scores into search results. A slow site is buried beneath faster competitors, no matter how good your product is.

**Revenue.** Studies consistently find that every extra second of load time drops conversions. On mobile — where most UAE traffic lives — patience is shorter still. A visitor who waits four seconds is often a visitor who's already gone.

## Why most sites fail them

Heavy templates, uncompressed images, third-party scripts stacked on top of each other, and fonts that block the page from rendering. Most of it is invisible to the owner until you measure it.
