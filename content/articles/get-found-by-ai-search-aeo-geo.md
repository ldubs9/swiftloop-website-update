# How to Get Your Business Found by AI Search (AEO / GEO)

Your customers have a new habit. Instead of searching and scrolling, they ask an AI — "who's the best jeweler in Dubai for custom rings?" — and trust the answer. If the AI doesn't know you, you don't exist in that conversation. Optimising for this is the new frontier, and most businesses haven't started.

## SEO, meet AEO and GEO

- **SEO** got you ranked on a results page.
- **AEO** (Answer Engine Optimization) and **GEO** (Generative Engine Optimization) get you *cited in the answer itself* — when an AI summarises, recommends or quotes.

The shift is from "be on page one" to "be the answer."

## How AI decides who to recommend

AI models pull from content they can read, trust and understand clearly. To be the source they cite:

- **Answer real questions directly.** Content structured around actual questions — like this article — is exactly what AI surfaces. Clear question, clear answer.
- **Be specific and factual.** Real numbers, real details, real expertise. Vague marketing fluff gets skipped; concrete facts get quoted.
- **Be consistent everywhere.** Your name, location, services and contact details should match across your site, listings and profiles. Consistency builds the confidence to cite you.
- **Be machine-readable.** Clean structure, clear headings, and signals AI tools look for.

## The llms.txt file

There's a new, simple convention: an `llms.txt` file at the root of your site — a plain summary of who you are and what you do, written for AI to read. It's a quiet, direct way of telling models the facts you want them to know. (SwiftLoop publishes one. Most businesses don't yet — which is exactly the opening.)

## Why act now

AI search is early. The businesses that structure their content and identity for it today are the ones AI will have learned to recommend by the time everyone else catches on. This is the same advantage early SEO adopters had a decade ago.
