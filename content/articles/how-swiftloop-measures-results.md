# How SwiftLoop Measures Results: Analytics, Reporting and Iteration

Plenty of studios will tell you a site looks great. Far fewer will show you what it *does*. We build to numbers — because a website is a business tool, and a tool's worth is measured, not admired. Here's how we close the loop with data.

## Measure outcomes, not vanity

Page views and "likes" feel good and mean little. The metrics that matter are the ones tied to your business:

- **Conversions** — enquiries, bookings, sales. The actual point of the site.
- **Conversion rate** — of the people who arrive, how many act. This is where design earns its keep.
- **Source quality** — which channels bring people who actually convert, not just visit.
- **Speed and Core Web Vitals** — because they directly shape everything above.
- **Drop-off points** — exactly where in the journey people leave, so we know what to fix.

## Reporting you can actually read

A report full of jargon helps no one. Ours says, in plain language: here's what happened, here's what it means, here's what we're changing because of it. Real numbers, a clear story, and a decision attached — never a data dump.

## Turning data into iteration

This is the part that compounds. Each month we read the numbers, form a clear hypothesis ("the enquiry form is losing people at the third field"), make a focused change, and measure whether it worked. Small, evidence-led improvements, repeated, add up to a site that performs dramatically better a year on than it did at launch.

## Why this needs the loop

A site measured once and left alone is a snapshot. A site measured and tuned every month is a system that learns. This is exactly why every build ships with a retainer — the iteration is where much of the value lives, and you can't iterate on a site nobody's watching.

## The honest promise

We won't promise numbers we can't measure. We promise to *measure* — to show you what the site does, to be straight about what's working and what isn't, and to keep tuning toward the outcomes that matter to your business. Designed by hand, run by machine, improved on the evidence.
