What's a Retainer — and Why Every SwiftLoop Build Ships With One

A website isn't a one-time purchase — it's a living system. Here's why we attach a care retainer to every build by default.

Most studios sell you a website, hand over the keys, and disappear. Six months later the site is out of date, something's broken, and you're a stranger trying to reach a developer who's moved on. We do it differently — every build ships with a Loop retainer by default. Here's why.

What a retainer actually is

A retainer is an ongoing monthly relationship: a set scope of care, improvement and support, for a predictable fee. It's not a "break-glass-in-emergency" contract. It's the assumption that the site will keep being tended — because the best sites are gardens, not statues.

Why a website needs tending

A website isn't furniture you buy once. It's a living system:

  • The web changes — browsers update, security patches matter, performance standards rise.
  • Your business changes — new products, new offers, new pages, new priorities.
  • The data talks — analytics reveal what's working and what isn't, but only if someone reads them and acts.
  • The automations evolve — AI prompts and workflows want tuning as the business shifts.

Left alone, even a beautiful site quietly decays. Tended, it compounds — getting faster, sharper and more effective every month.

Our tiers

We offer Care, Growth and Scale retainers, sized to how much the site is asked to do. The point isn't to lock you in — it's that a site without care is a depreciating asset, and we'd rather not build those.

Why "default," not "optional"

Because removing it is a downgrade, not a saving. We attach a retainer by default the same way a good car comes with servicing in mind. You can take the site and run it yourself — but the version of our work that actually performs over time is the version that's looked after.

The honest value

A retainer means a real person who knows your site is a message away. It means the site keeps earning instead of slowly fading. And it means the loop — measure, tune, improve — actually closes. That's the whole philosophy: designed by hand, run by machine, improved on repeat.
