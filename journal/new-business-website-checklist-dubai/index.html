Launching a New Business in Dubai: Your Digital Presence Checklist

Just set up a free zone or mainland company in Dubai? Here's the practical digital checklist to go from licence to a presence that actually wins customers.

You've got the trade licence — free zone or mainland — and now you need customers. In Dubai's fast, competitive market, a business without a credible digital presence barely exists. Here's the practical checklist to go from "registered" to "findable and trusted," in the right order.

1. Lock down the basics

  • Domain — ideally a clean .com and the matching .ae for local trust. Secure both before someone else does.
  • Business email on your domain (you@yourbrand.ae), not a free Gmail. It's the cheapest credibility you'll buy.
  • The name, consistent everywhere — exactly as on your licence, so listings and search line up.

2. A website that does a job

Skip the "online brochure" mindset. Decide what the site must do — generate enquiries, take bookings, sell — and build for that. For most new Dubai businesses, a sharp, fast, conversion-focused site beats a sprawling one. It should be bilingual where your customers are, and load instantly on mobile.

3. Get found locally

  • Google Business Profile — complete it fully. "Service near me" and "X in Dubai" searches are won here.
  • Core local listings with consistent details.
  • Basic SEO baked in — clear structure, real content answering what customers ask.

4. The channels customers expect here

In the UAE, that means WhatsApp front and centre — a clear way to message you, ideally with instant automated replies so no first enquiry goes cold while you're finding your feet.

5. Capture and follow up from day one

Early leads are precious. Wire the site so every enquiry is captured, acknowledged instantly, and followed up — even before you've hired anyone. A simple automation here can be the difference between a slow start and momentum.

6. Look the part

First impressions compound. Consistent branding across the site, your social and your documents (yes, even the invoice) signals a serious business, not a side project. In Dubai's referral-driven economy, looking established helps you become it.

The takeaway

A new Dubai business doesn't need everything on day one — it needs the right things, in order: a clean domain and email, a focused site, local findability, WhatsApp, and follow-up that never drops a lead. We help founders launch with a presence that punches above a startup's weight.
